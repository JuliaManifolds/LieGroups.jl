The semidirect product Lie group

The semidirect product has a few choices regarding left and right:

  • for the order of the product: LeftSemidirectProductLieGroup $\mathcal G ‚ãâ \mathcal H$ vs. RightSemidirectProductLieGroup $\mathcal H ‚ãä \mathcal G$
  • for the GroupAction $Œ±$: AbstractLeftGroupActionType $œÉ$ vs. AbstractRightGroupActionType $œÑ$
  • for the GroupAction $Œ±_g$ w.r.t. a fixed $g ‚àà \mathcal G$, within a group operation $h_1‚ãÑh_2$: ActionActsOnLeft $Œ±_g(h_1)‚ãÑh_2$ vs. ActionActsOnRight $h_1‚ãÑŒ±_g(h_2)$

These choices lead to different formulae, usually even all eight cases are different. We still try to document them.

Inverse in Semidirect Product Groups

Let $œÉ$ denote a left group action (<:AbstractLeftGroupActionType) and $œÑ$ a right group action (<:AbstractRightGroupActionType). Let AO be the type indicating whether the action is applied on the left (ActionActsOnLeft) or right (ActionActsOnRight).

The formulas for the inverse depend on whether the action act on the left or on the right as follows:

Left semidirect product (LeftSemidirectProductGroupOperation):

  • Acting on the left (AO <: ActionActsOnLeft):

\[(g, h)^{-1} = (g^{-1}, œÉ_{g}(h^{-1}))\]

\[(g, h)^{-1} = (g^{-1}, œÑ_{g^{-1}}(h^{-1}))\]

  • Acting on the right (AO <: ActionActsOnRight):

\[(g, h)^{-1} = (g^{-1}, œÉ_{g^{-1}}(h^{-1}))\]

\[(g, h)^{-1} = (g^{-1}, œÑ_{g}(h^{-1}))\]

Right semidirect product (RightSemidirectProductGroupOperation):

  • Acting on the left (AO <: ActionActsOnLeft):

\[(h, g)^{-1} = (œÉ_{g}(h^{-1}), g^{-1})\]

\[(h, g)^{-1} = (œÑ_{g^{-1}}(h^{-1}), g^{-1})\]

  • Acting on the right (AO <: ActionActsOnRight):

\[(h, g)^{-1} = (œÉ_{g^{-1}}(h^{-1}), g^{-1})\]

\[(h, g)^{-1} = (œÑ_{g}(h^{-1}), g^{-1})\]

Note:

  • The formulas above match the conventions in [HN12, Definition 9.2.22] with œÉ = Œ±.
  • The relationship between left and right actions is $œÉ_g := œÑ_{g^{-1}}$.

See also: AbstractLeftGroupActionType, AbstractRightGroupActionType, ActionActsOnLeft, ActionActsOnRight
