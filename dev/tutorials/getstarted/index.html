Introduction

This tutorial introduces both a few basics of Lie groups as well as how to use LieGroups.jl. The Lie groups we consider are the rotations in the plane, or special orthogonal group as well as rigid body motions, or in other words the special euclidean group.

In a nutshell a Lie group $ğ’¢$ is a manifold $â„³$ that also is a group with a group operation.

A manifold can informally be described as a set that â€œlocally looks like a Euclidean spaceâ€ and has some way to measure angles at every point. Formally, this yields an inner product that depends (smoothly) on the point one is at. For Lie groups this will be a bit easier.

A group means that on the set there also exists an operation, within this package usually denoted by $âˆ˜: ğ’¢ Ã— ğ’¢ â†’ ğ’¢$, LieGroups.jl uses the manifolds defined in Manifolds.jl. For more details on that, see also the introductory tutorial there. A Lie group is usually written as a tuple $(â„³,âˆ˜)$ with the manifold and the group operation thereon.

For more theoretical background, see for example [HN12], especially their Chapter 9.

Representing tangent vectors

Consider the function of left group composition $Î»_g(h) = gâˆ˜h$. Its differential (or push forward) $DÎ»_g(e)$ maps from $T_eğ’¢$ to $T_gğ’¢$ and is a linear bijection between both spaces. Its adjoint differential (or pullback) $D^*Î»_g(e)$ maps back from $T_gğ’¢$ to $T_eğ’¢$.

This allows to introduce an inner product on all tangent spaces, that smoothly varies with the base point, we obtain the left-invariant metric

\[âŸ¨X,YâŸ©_g = âŸ¨D^*Î»_g(e)[X],D^*Î»_g(e)âŸ©_e,\]

Alternatively one can start with the right composition and its differential to arrive an right-invariant vector fields and a right-invariant metric.

The left-invariant metric is in general not right-invariant, except for compact Lie groups and their direct products with translations, see for example [LT13] for an even more general proof.

\[\exp_g(X) = g âˆ˜ \exp_ğ’¢(X) \quad\text{with its inverse}\quad \log_g(h) = \log_ğ’¢(g^{-1}âˆ˜h)\]

Products and semidirect products of Lie groups

Similar to product manifolds (direct) product Lie groups are formed the same way. For two Lie groups $ğ’¢ = ğ’¢_1 Ã— ğ’¢_2$ is the product of the two manifolds together with the component wise application of the group operations: the first group operation (of $ğ’¢_1$) to the first component and that of $ğ’¢_2$ to the second.

Instead of the component wise or â€œnot interactingâ€ variant of the (direct) product Lie groups, for the semidirect product Lie groups $ğ’¢ = ğ’¢_1 â‹‰ ğ’¢_2$ we also require an action of how $ğ’¢_1$ acts on $ğ’¢_2$. Semidirect here means that the first component of $ğ’¢$ is the same as for the direct product, but before applying the second group operation on $ğ’¢_2$ one of the elements is â€œacted uponâ€ from an element of $ğ’¢_1$. The group operation reads for $(g_1,g_2), (h_1,h_2) âˆˆ ğ’¢$

\[(g_1,g_2) âˆ˜ (h_1,h_2) := (g_1 â‹† h_1, Ïƒ_{h_1}(g_2) â‹„ h_2).\]
